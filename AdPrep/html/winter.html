Ski Touring

Ski Touring requires lots of equipment that is very vital to your survival in nature. For this type of activity we strongly recommend you get well-informed on alpine terrain and conditions. The beauty of these snowy landscapes can be deceinvingly dangerous, it is important to know the types of dangers you can encounter at your location. Lastly, you must find a partner to accompany you, this is the only way you can have a chance of surviving an avalanche or a bad injury. Soon our website will offer the ability to try to find you a partner for any activity that could be dangerous alone.

Equipement

To ski tour you need all the pieces necessary to be able to climb the mountain before going down. Ski Touring requires a different kind of ski binding as well as a special kind of ski boots. Don't forget to bring skins, which are strips you put underneath your skies, which prevents from sliding down the hill. You must ensure you are familiar with how to use a beacon and compass to properly find your way up or down the mountain. This list is not a complete ski touring equipment list, this is because there are simply too many items that you should bring. Make sure you do extensive research for dangerous trips like these.

Hiking List

  • Touring Gear
  • Food and non perishable Snacks
  • 2L Water
  • Snow Pants
  • Winter Jacket
  • Goggles
  • Beacon
  • First Aid Kit
  • Map of Area
